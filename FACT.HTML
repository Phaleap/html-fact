The Fisheries Action Coalition Team (FACT) is a well-known non-governmental organization (NGO) based in Cambodia. Founded in 2000, FACT works to protect and promote the rights of local fishing communities, especially those living around Cambodia's major rivers and lakes, such as the Tonle Sap Lake, the Mekong River, and the coastal areas.

HOW ABOUT OUR ACHIEVEMENTS?

The FACT organization in Cambodia stands for Fisheries Action Coalition Team. It is a well-known Cambodian NGO (non-governmental organization) that focuses on protecting the country’s freshwater fisheries, especially around the Tonle Sap Lake, the Mekong River, and other important fishing areas. Here are some key achievements and areas FACT has contributed to: Community Empowerment: FACT has helped local fishing communities organize and advocate for their rights. They assist communities in setting up Community Fisheries (CFi) groups, which are recognized by law and allow local people to co-manage and protect fisheries resources. Combating Illegal Fishing: The organization has worked on monitoring and reporting illegal fishing activities, which helps protect the fish stock and biodiversity in Cambodia’s lakes and rivers. Policy Advocacy: FACT has been involved in pushing for fair and sustainable fisheries policies. They have participated in national discussions and lobbied for better laws to ensure that fishing practices do not harm the environment or local livelihoods. Environmental Conservation: They have taken part in protecting flooded forests and wetlands, which are crucial for fish spawning and the overall health of the aquatic ecosystem in Cambodia. Awareness and Education: FACT conducts training and awareness campaigns to educate communities and the public about sustainable fishing, environmental protection, and the importance of conserving natural resources.
